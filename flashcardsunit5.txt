Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) | Guidelines contained in Part IV (Articles 36-51) of the Constitution, non-justiciable but fundamental in governance, aiming to establish a welfare state.
Article 36 | Defines "State" for DPSP, adopting the same meaning as Article 12 (includes central/state governments, legislatures, local authorities, and other authorities).
Article 37 | Declares that DPSP are not enforceable by courts but are fundamental in governance; it is the duty of the State to apply them in making laws.
Socialist principles (DPSP) | DPSP focusing on economic justice, welfare state, reduction of inequality, including Articles 38, 39, 39A, 41, 42, 43, 47.
Gandhian principles (DPSP) | DPSP reflecting Mahatma Gandhi's ideas: village panchayats (Article 40), cottage industries (Article 43), welfare of weaker sections (Article 46), prohibition (Article 47), animal husbandry (Article 48).
Liberal-Intellectual principles (DPSP) | DPSP promoting progressive governance: Uniform Civil Code (Article 44), early childhood care (Article 45), environment (48A), monuments (49), separation of judiciary (50), international peace (51).
Article 38 | Directs the State to secure a social order for welfare of people, minimize inequalities in income, status, facilities, and opportunities.
Article 39 | Contains economic principles: adequate livelihood, common good of resources, no wealth concentration, equal pay for equal work, protection of workers and children.
Article 39A | Directs the State to promote equal justice and provide free legal aid to ensure that economic disabilities do not deny access to justice.
Article 40 | Directs the State to organize village panchayats as units of local self-government (Gandhian principle).
Article 41 | Directs the State to secure the right to work, education, and public assistance in cases of unemployment, old age, sickness, and disability.
Article 42 | Directs the State to provide just and humane conditions of work and maternity relief.
Article 43 | Directs the State to secure a living wage, decent standard of life, and social opportunities for workers; also promotes cottage industries.
Article 44 | Directs the State to secure a Uniform Civil Code for citizens throughout the territory of India (Liberal-Intellectual principle).
Article 45 | Directs the State to provide early childhood care and education for all children below the age of six years (modified by 86th Amendment).
Article 46 | Directs the State to promote educational and economic interests of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, and other weaker sections; protect them from social injustice.
Article 47 | Directs the State to raise the level of nutrition, standard of living, and improve public health; also to prohibit intoxicating drinks and drugs (except medicinal).
Article 48 | Directs the State to organize agriculture and animal husbandry on scientific lines; prohibit slaughter of cows, calves, and other milch animals.
Article 48A | Directs the State to protect and improve the environment, forests, and wildlife (added by 42nd Amendment).
Article 49 | Directs the State to protect monuments, places, and objects of national importance from destruction or disfigurement.
Article 50 | Directs the State to separate the judiciary from the executive in public services to ensure judicial independence.
Article 51 | Directs the State to promote international peace and security, maintain just relations, respect international law, and encourage settlement of disputes by arbitration.
Champakam Dorairajan case (1951) | Supreme Court held that Fundamental Rights prevail over Directive Principles because DPSP are non-justiciable; led to the First Amendment.
Kesavananda Bharati case (1973) | Supreme Court held that Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles are complementary; harmony between them is part of the basic structure of the Constitution.
Non-justiciable | Not enforceable by any court; a characteristic of DPSP, meaning individuals cannot approach courts for their violation.
Fundamental in governance | Phrase from Article 37 meaning that DPSP are essential guidelines for the State while making laws and policies, even though not enforceable.
