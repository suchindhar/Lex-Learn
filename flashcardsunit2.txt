What does the Preamble of the Indian Constitution represent? | The introductory statement reflecting the philosophy, objectives, and ideals of the Constitution, acting as a key to understand its spirit.
What is the source of authority according to the Preamble? | "We, the People of India" – the Constitution derives its authority from the people, establishing popular sovereignty.
What are the four major objectives of the Preamble? | Justice, Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity.
What three types of justice are mentioned in the Preamble? | Social justice, Economic justice, and Political justice.
What does "Sovereign" mean in the Preamble? | India is independent and not subject to any external authority; it has supreme power to make laws and conduct internal and external affairs.
What does "Socialist" mean in the Indian Preamble? | Democratic socialism – aims to reduce inequality, ensure social justice, and create a welfare state with equitable distribution of resources.
What does "Secular" mean in the Preamble? | The state has no religion of its own, treats all religions equally, and guarantees freedom to practice, profess, and propagate any religion.
What does "Democratic" mean in the Preamble? | Government formed by the people through elected representatives, based on universal adult franchise, rule of law, and accountability.
What does "Republic" mean in the Preamble? | The head of the state (President) is elected, not hereditary; no position is reserved for hereditary rulers.
What does "Liberty" guarantee under the Preamble? | Liberty of thought, expression, belief, faith, and worship.
What does "Equality" guarantee under the Preamble? | Equality of status and opportunity, prohibiting discrimination, while allowing reasonable classification and substantive equality for weaker sections.
What does "Fraternity" assure? | Brotherhood and unity among citizens, dignity of the individual, and unity and integrity of the nation.
When was the Constitution adopted and enforced? | Adopted on 26 November 1949; enforced on 26 January 1950.
Is the Preamble part of the Constitution? | Yes, as held in Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973) and LIC of India Case (1995), the Preamble is an integral part of the Constitution.
Can the Preamble be amended? | Yes, under Article 368, but the amendment cannot destroy the basic structure of the Constitution (Kesavananda Bharati).
What was the Berubari Union Case (1960) view on the Preamble? | Initially held that the Preamble is not a part of the Constitution, only a key to understand the framers' minds – later overruled.
What is the interpretative value of the Preamble? | Courts refer to the Preamble to resolve ambiguity in constitutional provisions and to understand the intention of the framers.
What words were added by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment (1976)? | Socialist, Secular, and Integrity.
