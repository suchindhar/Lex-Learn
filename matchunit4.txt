Article 12 | Defines "State" for Fundamental Rights.
Article 13 | Laws inconsistent with Fundamental Rights are void; judicial review.
Doctrine of severability | Only the unconstitutional part of a law is void; valid part remains.
Doctrine of eclipse | Pre-constitutional law becomes dormant but may revive.
Article 14 | Equality before law and equal protection of laws.
Reasonable classification | Requires intelligible differentia and rational nexus.
Article 15 | Prohibits discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth.
Article 16 | Equality of opportunity in public employment.
Article 17 | Abolishes untouchability.
Article 18 | Abolishes titles except military and academic.
Article 19 | Six freedoms: speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession.
Article 20 | Protection against ex post facto law, double jeopardy, self-incrimination.
Article 21 | Protection of life and personal liberty; expanded by Maneka Gandhi case.
Article 21A | Right to education for children aged 6-14 years (86th Amendment).
Article 22 | Protection against arbitrary arrest and preventive detention safeguards.
Article 23 | Prohibits human trafficking, begar, forced labour.
Article 24 | Prohibits child labour below 14 in hazardous industries.
Article 25 | Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice, propagation of religion.
Article 30 | Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions.
Article 32 | Right to constitutional remedies; writ jurisdiction of Supreme Court.
