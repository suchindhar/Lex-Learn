UNIT 2 – PREAMBLE OF THE CONSTITUTION
The Preamble is the introductory statement of the Constitution of India. It reflects the philosophy, objectives, and ideals of the Constitution. It explains the source of authority of the Constitution and the goals that the Constitution seeks to achieve. The Preamble acts as a key to understand the spirit of the Constitution. It expresses the intention of the framers and the values on which the constitutional structure is based.
The Preamble begins with the words “We, the People of India”. This indicates that the Constitution derives its authority from the people. It shows that sovereignty lies with the people and not with any external authority. The people of India gave themselves the Constitution through the Constituent Assembly. Thus, the Preamble reflects popular sovereignty and democratic legitimacy.
The Preamble also declares India to be Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic. It further aims to secure justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity. These values form the foundation of constitutional governance. The Preamble therefore serves as a guiding principle for interpretation of the Constitution.
________________________________________
Meaning of the Preamble
The word “Preamble” means introduction or preface. In constitutional law, it refers to the opening part of the Constitution which explains its objectives and philosophy. The Preamble does not grant powers or impose limitations directly, but it helps in understanding the intention behind constitutional provisions.
The Preamble outlines:
•	Source of authority (people) 
•	Nature of the state 
•	Objectives of the Constitution 
•	Ideals to be achieved 
Thus, the Preamble acts as a summary of the Constitution. It explains why the Constitution was created and what goals it intends to accomplish.
________________________________________
Illustration
If a book has an introduction, it explains what the book is about. Similarly, the Preamble explains what the Constitution aims to achieve. It is like a guiding note before reading the Constitution.
________________________________________
Nature of the Preamble
The nature of the Preamble has been debated in constitutional law. The question was whether the Preamble is part of the Constitution or merely an introductory statement. Initially, there was uncertainty about its legal status. However, later judicial interpretation clarified its importance.
The Preamble is not a source of power. It does not confer rights or duties. But it plays an important interpretative role. When constitutional provisions are ambiguous, courts refer to the Preamble to understand the intention of the framers. Thus, the Preamble acts as a guiding tool.
The Preamble reflects basic values such as democracy, equality, liberty, and justice. These values form the foundation of constitutional structure. Therefore, the Preamble is considered an integral part of constitutional philosophy.
________________________________________
Example
If a constitutional provision is unclear, courts may refer to the Preamble to understand whether the provision promotes equality or liberty. This helps in interpretation.

Thus, the Preamble is the key to understanding the Constitution.
The Preamble to the Constitution of India sets out the objectives that the Constitution seeks to achieve. These objectives reflect the vision of the framers and the values that guide governance. The Preamble not only declares the nature of the state but also outlines the goals of political, social, and economic justice. It ensures that the Constitution is not merely a legal document but a statement of national aspirations.
The objectives of the Preamble are to establish a sovereign democratic republic and secure justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity for all citizens. These objectives shape the interpretation of constitutional provisions. They also guide the legislature, executive, and judiciary in performing their functions. Thus, the Preamble acts as a guiding principle for governance.

Objectives of the Preamble
The Preamble sets out four major objectives:
•	Justice 
•	Liberty 
•	Equality 
•	Fraternity 
These objectives are interconnected. Justice ensures fairness in society. Liberty guarantees freedom to individuals. Equality ensures equal treatment before law. Fraternity promotes unity and brotherhood. Together, these objectives aim to create a democratic welfare state.
The objective of justice includes social, economic, and political justice. Social justice aims to eliminate discrimination and inequality. Economic justice aims to reduce wealth disparities. Political justice ensures equal participation in governance.
Liberty ensures freedom of thought, expression, belief, faith, and worship. This protects individual autonomy. Equality ensures equality before law and equal opportunity. Fraternity promotes unity and dignity of individuals.
Thus, the Preamble reflects the vision of a democratic society based on equality and justice.

Source of Authority – “We the People”
The Preamble begins with the words “We, the People of India”. This phrase indicates that the Constitution derives authority from the people. It establishes popular sovereignty. The government derives power from the people and functions on their behalf.
This concept reflects democratic legitimacy. The Constitution was adopted by representatives of the people in the Constituent Assembly. Therefore, ultimate authority lies with citizens. No external authority controls the Constitution.
The phrase also indicates that India is a sovereign state. The people themselves gave the Constitution. Thus, the Constitution reflects the will of the people.

Date of Adoption
The Preamble also mentions the date of adoption of the Constitution. The Constitution was adopted on 26 November 1949. On this date, the Constituent Assembly finalized the Constitution. However, the Constitution came into force on 26 January 1950.
The date 26 January was chosen to commemorate the declaration of Purna Swaraj in 1930. Thus, adoption and enforcement dates differ.
Adoption → 26 November 1949
Enforcement → 26 January 1950
This distinction is important in constitutional law.

The Preamble of the Constitution of India declares India to be a Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic. These words describe the nature of the Indian state. They define the political identity of India and the principles on which governance is based. Among these, Sovereign, Socialist, and Secular explain independence, social justice, and religious neutrality.
________________________________________
Sovereign
The term “Sovereign” means that India is independent and not subject to any external authority. It implies that India has supreme power to make laws and conduct its internal and external affairs. No foreign country can control India’s decisions. Sovereignty also means that India can enter into treaties, conduct foreign relations, and determine its own policies.
Before independence, India was under British rule. The British Parliament had authority over Indian governance. After adoption of the Constitution, sovereignty transferred to the people of India. The government derives authority from the people. Thus, sovereignty lies with the people.
Sovereignty also includes internal and external sovereignty. Internal sovereignty means the state has supreme authority within its territory. External sovereignty means the state is free from foreign control. India exercises both.
India is also a member of international organizations like the United Nations. However, this does not affect sovereignty because membership is voluntary. India retains power to make independent decisions.
________________________________________
Example
India can sign international agreements, declare policies, and enact laws without seeking permission from any other country. This shows sovereignty.
________________________________________
Illustration
Before 1947 → British Parliament controlled India
After Constitution → People of India control governance
This shows sovereign status.
________________________________________
Socialist
The word “Socialist” was added to the Preamble by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment in 1976. Socialism in Indian context does not mean complete state ownership. It refers to democratic socialism. It aims to reduce inequality and ensure social justice.
The concept of socialism in India promotes welfare state. The state works to reduce economic disparities. It provides opportunities for all citizens. Policies such as land reforms, poverty reduction, and social welfare reflect socialist ideals.
Socialism also means equitable distribution of resources. The state regulates economic activity to prevent concentration of wealth. Directive Principles of State Policy reflect socialist philosophy.
Indian socialism balances public and private sectors. It does not eliminate private property. Instead, it regulates economic development to promote social welfare.
________________________________________
Example
Government schemes for education, healthcare, and poverty reduction reflect socialist principles. These aim to reduce inequality.
________________________________________
Illustration

Secular
The term “Secular” means that the state has no religion of its own. The state treats all religions equally. It does not favour any religion. Every citizen has freedom to practice, profess, and propagate religion.
Secularism in India differs from western secularism. Indian secularism means equal respect for all religions. The state may regulate religious practices to maintain public order. But it cannot discriminate based on religion.
The Constitution guarantees religious freedom under Articles 25 to 28. These provisions ensure that individuals can practice religion freely. The state cannot impose any religion.
Secularism also promotes unity in diversity. India has multiple religions. Secularism ensures peaceful coexistence.
________________________________________
Example
Government does not declare any official religion. Citizens can follow Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, or any religion freely. This reflects secularism.

The Preamble of the Constitution of India declares India to be a Democratic Republic. These two words describe the form of government adopted in India. Democracy refers to government by the people, while Republic refers to an elected head of state. Together, these concepts ensure representative government and political equality.
________________________________________
Democratic
The word “Democratic” means that the government is formed by the people and functions according to their will. In a democratic system, people elect their representatives. These representatives form the government. Thus, ultimate authority lies with the people. Democracy ensures political participation and accountability.
Indian democracy is based on universal adult franchise. Every citizen above 18 years has the right to vote irrespective of caste, religion, gender, or wealth. This ensures political equality. Elections are conducted periodically. The government remains accountable to the people.
Democracy also implies rule of law. All persons including government officials are subject to law. It also ensures protection of fundamental rights. Freedom of speech, equality before law, and liberty are essential for democracy.
India follows parliamentary democracy. The executive is responsible to legislature. The Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to Lok Sabha. If the government loses majority, it must resign. This ensures accountability.
Democracy also includes political, social, and economic dimensions. Political democracy ensures voting rights. Social democracy ensures equality. Economic democracy aims to reduce inequalities.
________________________________________
Example
Citizens vote in elections → representatives elected → government formed.
If people are dissatisfied → they vote out government.
This is democratic governance.
________________________________________
Illustration

This is democratic system.

Republic
The word “Republic” means that the head of the state is elected and not hereditary. In monarchy, the head of state inherits position by birth. In republic, the head of state is elected for a fixed term. India adopted republican form of government.
In India, the President is the head of state. The President is elected by an electoral college consisting of elected members of Parliament and state legislatures. The President holds office for five years. This reflects republican principle.
Republic also implies political equality. All citizens are eligible to hold public office. No position is reserved for hereditary rulers. This promotes equality.
The republic form replaced British monarchy. Before independence, the British monarch was head of state. After adoption of Constitution, the President became head of state. Thus, India became a republic.
________________________________________
Example
India’s President is elected, not born into office. Any eligible citizen can become President. This reflects republican system.

The Constitution promotes social justice through various provisions. Fundamental Rights prohibit discrimination. Equality before law ensures equal treatment. Abolition of untouchability promotes social equality. Reservation policies also aim to promote social justice.
Social justice also ensures protection of weaker sections. The Constitution provides safeguards for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, and other backward classes. These measures reduce historical disadvantages.

The Preamble of the Constitution of India declares that the Constitution seeks to secure Equality of status and opportunity and promote Fraternity assuring dignity of the individual and unity and integrity of the nation. Equality
Equality is one of the core objectives of the Constitution. The Preamble guarantees equality of status and equality of opportunity. Equality of status means all individuals are treated equally in society. No person is superior or inferior based on caste, religion, gender, or birth. Equality of opportunity means every person has equal chance in education, employment, and public life.
The Constitution promotes equality through Fundamental Rights. Equality before law ensures that all persons are subject to same law. Equal protection of laws ensures fairness. The Constitution prohibits discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth. It also guarantees equal opportunity in public employment.
Equality does not mean absolute equality. Reasonable classification is allowed. The state can make special provisions for weaker sections. Reservation for backward classes is allowed to achieve real equality. This is called substantive equality.
Thus, equality ensures fairness and removes discrimination. It promotes inclusive society.

The legal status of the Preamble to the Constitution of India has been a subject of constitutional debate. The main question was whether the Preamble is part of the Constitution or merely an introductory statement. This issue became important because if the Preamble is part of the Constitution, it can influence interpretation of constitutional provisions. Courts examined this issue in various cases and clarified its status.
________________________________________
Whether Preamble is Part of the Constitution
Initially, the Preamble was not considered a part of the Constitution. It was treated as a guiding statement. The view was that the Preamble does not confer powers or impose limitations. Therefore, it was considered non-justiciable. However, later judicial interpretation changed this view.
The modern position is that the Preamble is part of the Constitution. Though it does not grant enforceable rights, it reflects the basic structure and philosophy of the Constitution. Courts can rely on the Preamble to interpret ambiguous provisions. Thus, the Preamble forms part of constitutional framework.
The Preamble cannot override express provisions of the Constitution. If the language of a provision is clear, the Preamble cannot change its meaning. However, if ambiguity exists, the Preamble helps interpret intention. Thus, it acts as a guiding tool.
________________________________________
Illustration
If constitutional provision is unclear → Court refers to Preamble
If provision is clear → Preamble cannot override
This shows interpretative role.

The Preamble also helps understand constitutional spirit. It explains democratic nature, secular character, and socialist orientation. It ensures that constitutional interpretation aligns with these values.

The Preamble also helps identify basic structure of the Constitution. Concepts such as sovereignty, democracy, secularism, and republic are part of basic structure. These cannot be destroyed by amendment.
The interpretative role of Preamble ensures that constitutional provisions are read harmoniously. It prevents narrow interpretation. It promotes constitutional ideals.

The Preamble of the Constitution of India plays an important role in understanding constitutional philosophy. A major constitutional question arose whether the Preamble can be amended. Since the Preamble reflects the basic ideals of the Constitution, the issue of amendment became significant. The Supreme Court clarified this issue through various landmark judgments.
________________________________________
Amendment of the Preamble
Originally, the Preamble did not contain the words Socialist, Secular, and Integrity. These words were inserted by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976. This amendment added:
•	Socialist 
•	Secular 
•	Integrity 
Thus, the Preamble was amended for the first time. This confirmed that the Preamble can be amended under Article 368. However, amendment cannot destroy basic structure of the Constitution.
The amendment reflected the evolving nature of the Constitution. The addition of socialist emphasized welfare state. The addition of secular reaffirmed religious neutrality. The word integrity strengthened national unity.
________________________________________
Illustration
Original Preamble:
Sovereign Democratic Republic
After 42nd Amendment:
Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic
Unity → Unity and Integrity
This shows amendment.
________________________________________
Berubari Union Case (1960)
Facts:
The Government of India entered into an agreement with Pakistan to transfer Berubari territory. The question arose whether such transfer could be made under executive power. The issue was referred to the Supreme Court for opinion.
Judgment:
The Supreme Court held that the Preamble is not a part of the Constitution. It stated that the Preamble is only a key to open the minds of framers. It cannot be used as source of power. Therefore, constitutional amendment was required to transfer territory.
Significance:
This case initially held that Preamble is not part of the Constitution. However, this view was later reconsidered.
________________________________________
Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973)
Facts:
The validity of constitutional amendments affecting fundamental rights was challenged. The issue before the Supreme Court was whether Parliament has unlimited power to amend the Constitution.
Judgment:
The Supreme Court held that the Preamble is part of the Constitution. The Court also held that Parliament can amend the Constitution but cannot destroy basic structure. The Preamble reflects basic structure such as democracy, secularism, and sovereignty.
Significance:
This case overruled the earlier view in Berubari case. It established that the Preamble is part of the Constitution and amendment cannot destroy its basic features.
________________________________________
LIC of India Case (1995)
Facts:
In this case, the interpretation of constitutional provisions relating to state policy was questioned. The court examined the role of the Preamble.
Judgment:
The Supreme Court held that the Preamble is integral part of the Constitution. It emphasized that the Preamble reflects the objectives of the Constitution. It should guide interpretation of constitutional provisions.
Significance:
This case reaffirmed the importance of the Preamble and its interpretative value.
