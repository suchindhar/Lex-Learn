Berubari Union Case (1960)
Facts: The Government of India entered into an agreement with Pakistan to transfer part of the Berubari territory. The question arose whether this transfer could be done by executive action alone or required a constitutional amendment.
Judgment: The Supreme Court held that cession of Indian territory to a foreign country cannot be done by executive action. Since territory is part of India under Article 1, any cession requires a constitutional amendment under Article 368 of the Constitution.
Significance: This case clarified the distinction between acquisition and cession of territory. Acquisition of territory can be done by executive action, but cession (transfer of territory) requires a constitutional amendment. It reinforced that the Union of India is indestructible and territorial integrity cannot be altered by ordinary law.
