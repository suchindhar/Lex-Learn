We, the People of India | Indicates that the Constitution derives its authority from the people and establishes popular sovereignty.
Sovereign | India is independent and not subject to any external authority; can make its own laws and policies.
Socialist | Added by 42nd Amendment (1976); refers to democratic socialism aiming to reduce inequality and ensure social justice.
Secular | State has no official religion; treats all religions equally; guarantees freedom of religion.
Democratic | Government by the people through elected representatives; based on universal adult franchise and accountability.
Republic | Head of state (President) is elected, not hereditary; no position reserved for hereditary rulers.
Justice (Social, Economic, Political) | Social removes discrimination, economic reduces wealth disparities, political ensures equal participation in governance.
Liberty | Guarantees freedom of thought, expression, belief, faith, and worship.
Equality | Equality of status and opportunity; prohibits discrimination but allows reasonable classification for weaker sections.
Fraternity | Promotes brotherhood, dignity of the individual, and unity and integrity of the nation.
26 November 1949 | Date of adoption of the Constitution by the Constituent Assembly.
26 January 1950 | Date of enforcement of the Constitution; chosen to commemorate Purna Swaraj declaration of 1930.
42nd Constitutional Amendment (1976) | Added the words Socialist, Secular, and Integrity to the Preamble.
Berubari Union Case (1960) | Initially held that Preamble is not part of the Constitution; later overruled by Kesavananda Bharati.
Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973) | Held that Preamble is part of the Constitution and reflects basic structure; amendment cannot destroy basic features.
LIC of India Case (1995) | Reaffirmed that Preamble is an integral part of the Constitution and guides interpretation.
Basic Structure Doctrine | Derived from Kesavananda Bharati; Preamble reflects basic features like democracy, secularism, sovereignty that cannot be destroyed by amendment.
Preamble's interpretative role | Courts use Preamble to resolve ambiguity in constitutional provisions and understand framers' intention.
