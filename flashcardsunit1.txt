Regulating Act 1773 | First British parliamentary Act to regulate East India Company | Introduced centralized administration, Supreme Court at Calcutta, and Executive Council.
Pitt's India Act 1784 | Established Board of Control and dual government | British government controlled political affairs; Company managed commerce.
Charter Act 1813 | Ended Company's trade monopoly with India | Allowed private British merchants, spent funds on education, permitted missionaries.
Charter Act 1833 | Made Governor-General of India, centralized legislation | Abolished Company's commercial functions, introduced open civil services, created Law Member.
Charter Act 1853 | Separated executive and legislative functions of Governor-General's council | Created separate legislative council, introduced competitive exams for civil services.
Government of India Act 1858 | Transferred power from Company to Crown | Began Crown rule, created Secretary of State for India, Viceroy replaced Governor-General.
Indian Councils Act 1861 | First Indian participation in law-making | Nominated Indians to councils, restored legislative powers to provinces, introduced portfolio system.
Indian Councils Act 1892 | Introduced indirect election and budget discussion | Expanded councils, allowed members to discuss budget but not vote.
Morley-Minto Reforms 1909 | Introduced separate electorates for Muslims | Expanded councils, included Indians in executive councils, first elections.
Government of India Act 1919 | Introduced dyarchy in provinces and bicameral legislature at centre | Reserved and transferred subjects; Council of State and Legislative Assembly.
Government of India Act 1935 | Most comprehensive pre-independence constitutional Act | Proposed federal structure, provincial autonomy, three lists, Federal Court.
Federal Court of India 1937 | First constitutional court in India | Had original, appellate and advisory jurisdiction; resolved centre-province disputes.
Indian Independence Act 1947 | Ended British rule and created India and Pakistan | Gave legislative sovereignty to Constituent Assembly, abolished Secretary of State.
Constituent Assembly | Body that framed Indian Constitution | First met 9 Dec 1946; had 299 members after partition; adopted Constitution 26 Nov 1949.
Drafting Committee | Committee chaired by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar | Prepared draft Constitution based on other committees' reports.
Objective Resolution | Moved by Jawaharlal Nehru on 13 Dec 1946 | Laid down philosophy of Constitution; became basis of Preamble.
Nand Kumar Case 1775 | Conflict between Supreme Court and executive | Nand Kumar accused Warren Hastings of corruption; tried under English law and sentenced to death; exposed jurisdictional confusion.
Dyarchy | Dual government system under 1919 Act | Reserved subjects (police, finance) by Governor; transferred subjects (education, health) by Indian ministers.
Separate electorates | Introduced by Morley-Minto Reforms 1909 | Only Muslim voters could elect Muslim representatives.
Preamble of Indian Constitution | Derived from Objective Resolution | Declares India sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic; based on justice, liberty, equality, fraternity.
