Fundamental Duties | Moral obligations of citizens contained in Part IVA (Article 51A) of the Constitution, added by the 42nd Amendment (1976), non-justiciable but guide responsible citizenship.
Article 51A | The sole article in Part IVA listing eleven Fundamental Duties of every citizen of India.
42nd Amendment (1976) | The constitutional amendment that added Part IVA and Fundamental Duties to the Constitution.
86th Amendment (2002) | The amendment that added Article 51A(k) making it the duty of parents/guardians to provide education to children aged 6-14.
Article 51A(a) | Duty to abide by the Constitution and respect its ideals, institutions, the National Flag, and the National Anthem.
Article 51A(b) | Duty to uphold and protect the sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India.
Article 51A(c) | Duty to defend the country and render national service when called upon.
Article 51A(d) | Duty to promote harmony and the spirit of common brotherhood transcending religious, linguistic, regional, or sectional diversities; to renounce practices derogatory to women's dignity.
Article 51A(e) | Duty to renounce practices derogatory to the dignity of women.
Article 51A(f) | Duty to value and preserve the rich heritage of India's composite culture.
Article 51A(g) | Duty to protect and improve the natural environment (forests, lakes, rivers, wildlife) and have compassion for living creatures.
Article 51A(h) | Duty to develop scientific temper, humanism, and the spirit of inquiry and reform.
Article 51A(i) | Duty to safeguard public property and to abjure violence.
Article 51A(j) | Duty to strive towards excellence in all spheres of individual and collective activity so that the nation constantly rises to higher levels of endeavour and achievement.
Article 51A(k) | Duty of parents or guardians to provide opportunities for education to their child or ward between the age of six and fourteen years (added by 86th Amendment).
Non-justiciable | Not enforceable by any court; Fundamental Duties are non-justiciable, meaning citizens cannot be compelled by court order to perform them directly.
Moral obligation | A duty based on ethical principles rather than legal compulsion; Fundamental Duties are moral obligations guiding citizen behaviour.
Relationship with Fundamental Rights | Fundamental Duties complement Fundamental Rights; rights and responsibilities are two sides of responsible citizenship.
Scientific temper | A rational, questioning, and evidence-based approach to knowledge and problems, encouraged under Article 51A(h).
M.C. Mehta case (1986) | Landmark case where the Supreme Court used Article 51A(g) (duty to protect environment) to strengthen environmental jurisprudence and impose obligations on polluters.
