UNIT 6 – FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES
Fundamental Duties are contained in Part IVA of the Constitution of India. These duties are provided under Article 51A. Fundamental Duties were added to the Constitution to remind citizens of their responsibilities towards the nation. While Fundamental Rights give freedoms to individuals, Fundamental Duties impose obligations. They ensure that citizens contribute to national development.
Originally, the Constitution did not contain Fundamental Duties. The framers focused mainly on Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles. Later, it was felt that citizens should also have responsibilities. Therefore, Fundamental Duties were introduced.
________________________________________
Background of Fundamental Duties
Fundamental Duties were added by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976. This amendment inserted Part IVA and Article 51A. Initially, ten Fundamental Duties were added. Later, one more duty was added by the 86th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2002. Thus, there are now eleven Fundamental Duties.
The idea of Fundamental Duties was inspired by socialist constitutions. It was believed that citizens should respect national values. Duties promote discipline and patriotism. They strengthen unity.
Fundamental Duties act as moral obligations. Though not enforceable, they guide behaviour of citizens. They also help courts interpret laws.

Article 51A – List of Fundamental Duties
Article 51A lists Fundamental Duties of citizens. These duties require citizens to:
•	Respect Constitution 
•	Respect national symbols 
•	Protect sovereignty 
•	Promote harmony 
•	Preserve culture 
•	Protect environment 
•	Develop scientific temper 
•	Safeguard public property 
•	Strive for excellence 
•	Provide education to children 
These duties apply to all citizens.
________________________________________
Purpose of Fundamental Duties
Fundamental Duties aim to:
•	Promote patriotism 
•	Maintain unity 
•	Protect environment 
•	Preserve culture 
•	Encourage discipline 
•	Strengthen democracy 
These duties remind citizens that rights come with responsibilities.

Article 51A of the Constitution of India lists Fundamental Duties of citizens. These duties remind citizens to respect national values and maintain unity. The first three duties focus on respect for Constitution, national symbols, and sovereignty of India.
These duties aim to promote patriotism and national integrity.
________________________________________
Article 51A(a) – Respect Constitution, National Flag and National Anthem
Article 51A(a) provides that every citizen shall abide by the Constitution and respect its ideals and institutions. Citizens must also respect the National Flag and National Anthem. This duty promotes respect for constitutional values.
Respecting the Constitution means following laws and democratic principles. Citizens must uphold justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity. They should respect institutions like Parliament, judiciary, and executive.
Respect for national flag and anthem promotes unity. These symbols represent national identity. Disrespect towards them weakens national integrity.
________________________________________
Example
Standing during National Anthem reflects respect under Article 51A(a).
________________________________________
Illustration
Respect Constitution → follow laws
Respect flag → national unity
This is Article 51A(a).
________________________________________
Article 51A(b) – Uphold Sovereignty, Unity and Integrity
Article 51A(b) states that every citizen shall uphold and protect sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India. This duty requires citizens to protect national unity. Citizens must not engage in activities that divide the nation.
Sovereignty means independence of India. Unity means togetherness of states. Integrity means territorial wholeness. Citizens must promote these values.
This duty is important in diverse country like India. Citizens must respect unity despite differences.
________________________________________
Example
Promoting national unity and avoiding divisive activities reflects Article 51A(b).
________________________________________
Article 51A(c) – Defend the Country
Article 51A(c) provides that every citizen shall defend the country and render national service when called upon. This duty requires citizens to support national defence. During emergency or war, citizens must serve nation.
This duty promotes patriotism. Citizens must be ready to contribute to defence. This may include military service or other national service.
________________________________________
Example
Serving in armed forces reflects duty under Article 51A(c).
________________________________________

Article 51A of the Constitution of India lays down Fundamental Duties of citizens. Clauses (d) to (f) focus on promoting harmony, respecting women, and preserving cultural heritage. These duties aim to maintain social unity and protect cultural identity.
________________________________________
Article 51A(d) – Promote Harmony and Brotherhood
Article 51A(d) provides that every citizen shall promote harmony and spirit of common brotherhood. Citizens must transcend religious, linguistic, regional, and sectional differences. This duty promotes unity in diversity.
India is a diverse country. People belong to different religions, languages, and cultures. This duty encourages citizens to live peacefully. It promotes tolerance and mutual respect.
This duty also requires citizens to avoid hatred. Social harmony strengthens national integration.
________________________________________

Respecting people of different religions reflects Article 51A(d).

Article 51A(e) – Renounce Practices Derogatory to Women
Article 51A(e) requires citizens to renounce practices derogatory to dignity of women. This duty promotes gender equality. Citizens must avoid customs that disrespect women.
Practices like discrimination, dowry, and violence against women violate dignity. This duty encourages elimination of such practices. It promotes respect for women.
This provision reflects constitutional commitment to equality.

Article 51A(f) – Preserve Cultural Heritage
Article 51A(f) provides that citizens must value and preserve rich heritage of Indian culture. India has diverse cultural traditions. This duty encourages citizens to protect heritage.
Cultural heritage includes monuments, traditions, language, art, and literature. Citizens should preserve them. This duty promotes cultural identity.
Preservation of heritage ensures continuity of history.

Article 51A(g) provides that every citizen shall protect and improve natural environment. This includes forests, lakes, rivers, and wildlife. Citizens must also have compassion for living creatures.
This duty promotes environmental protection. Pollution harms society. Citizens must avoid activities damaging environment. Planting trees, conserving water, and protecting wildlife reflect this duty.
This provision complements Directive Principles relating to environment.

UNIT 6 – FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES
Article 51A(k) of the Constitution of India introduces an important Fundamental Duty relating to education. This clause was inserted by the 86th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2002. It requires parents and guardians to provide opportunities for education to children.
Article 51A(k) states that it shall be the duty of every parent or guardian to provide opportunities for education to his child or ward between the age of six and fourteen years. This duty complements the right to education under Article 21A. While Article 21A imposes duty on the State, Article 51A(k) imposes duty on parents.
Thus, education becomes joint responsibility of State and parents.
________________________________________
Purpose of Article 51A(k)
The purpose of this duty is to ensure universal education. Children must receive basic education. Parents must send children to school. This reduces illiteracy and child labour.
Education is essential for development. Without parental support, children may not attend school. This duty encourages parents to prioritize education.
It also strengthens fundamental right to education. The State provides schools, but parents must ensure attendance.
________________________________________
Example
Parents sending children to school reflect Article 51A(k).
________________________________________
Relationship with Article 21A
Article 21A provides free and compulsory education. Article 51A(k) creates duty of parents. Both provisions work together.
Article 21A → duty of State
Article 51A(k) → duty of parents
Together they ensure education for children.
________________________________________
Illustration
State provides schools → parents send children → education ensured
This reflects Article 51A(k).
________________________________________
Importance of Educational Duty
This duty:
•	Promotes literacy 
•	Reduces child labour 
•	Ensures development 
•	Strengthens democracy 
•	Improves society 
Education helps children become responsible citizens.
________________________________________
Conceptual Importance

UNIT 6 – FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES
Fundamental Duties under Article 51A of the Constitution of India impose responsibilities on citizens. These duties promote discipline, unity, and patriotism. Though they are not enforceable by courts, they play an important role in constitutional governance.
________________________________________
Nature of Fundamental Duties
Fundamental Duties are non-justiciable. This means courts cannot directly enforce them. A citizen cannot be punished solely for not performing a duty. However, Parliament may enact laws to implement duties. Violation of such laws may attract punishment.
Fundamental Duties are moral obligations. They guide behaviour of citizens. They promote national values. These duties apply only to citizens.
Fundamental Duties:
•	Non-justiciable 
•	Moral obligations 
•	Applicable to citizens 
•	Promote discipline 
Thus, they encourage responsible citizenship.
________________________________________
Importance of Fundamental Duties
Fundamental Duties are important because they:
•	Promote patriotism 
•	Maintain national unity 
•	Protect environment 
•	Preserve culture 
•	Encourage scientific temper 
•	Promote education 
•	Safeguard public property 
These duties strengthen democracy. Citizens must respect Constitution and national symbols. Duties balance rights.
________________________________________
Illustration
Rights → freedoms
Duties → responsibilities
Balance → good citizenship
________________________________________
Fundamental Rights vs Fundamental Duties
Fundamental Rights give freedoms to citizens. Fundamental Duties impose responsibilities. Rights and duties are complementary. One cannot exist without the other.
Fundamental Rights:
•	Enforceable 
•	Against State 
•	Protect liberty 
Fundamental Duties:
•	Non-enforceable 
•	Moral obligations 
•	Promote responsibility 
Both together create balanced society.
________________________________________
Example
Freedom of speech (Right) must be exercised responsibly (Duty).
________________________________________
Role of Courts
Courts may consider Fundamental Duties while interpreting laws. Duties help courts uphold constitutional values. They may justify reasonable restrictions.
Thus, though non-justiciable, duties influence legal interpretation.
________________________________________
Conclusion of Unit 6
Fundamental Duties were added to promote responsible citizenship. They remind citizens of obligations towards nation. These duties include respect for Constitution, unity, environment, and education. They complement Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles. Together, they strengthen democratic system.
